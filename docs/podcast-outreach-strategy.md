# Podcast Outreach Strategy — ChurnRecovery

_Last updated: 2026-03-21_

## Goal
Get booked on 3–5 podcast episodes in the creator economy / indie business space to drive waitlist signups and build brand awareness before the Product Hunt launch.

---

## Target Shows

### 1. Creator Science (Jay Clouse)
- **Audience size:** ~40,000 listeners/episode; strong newsletter audience of 8,000+
- **Topic fit:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Covers creator monetization, newsletters, audience-first businesses. ChurnRecovery is creator infrastructure.
- **Contact method:** Twitter DM (@jayclouse) after engaging on posts. Also has a guest submission form linked in show notes.
- **Best angle:** "Creator Economy Infrastructure" — Jay talks about systems creators need to build sustainable businesses. Pitch: "The cancel moment is where creator revenue evaporates silently. I built a free tool that handles it — and the gap it fills says something about how underserved creator businesses are."
- **Warm-up:** Reply thoughtfully to Jay's newsletter posts. Comment on his Twitter threads about creator monetization. At least 2 weeks of engagement before pitching.

### 2. The Indie Bites (James McKinven)
- **Audience size:** ~5,000–8,000 listeners/episode; tight-knit bootstrapper community
- **Topic fit:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Short-form interviews with bootstrapped founders. Perfect format for a "I built a free tool because I couldn't afford the expensive one" story.
- **Contact method:** Twitter DM (@JamesMcKinven) is preferred. Very accessible host.
- **Best angle:** "Free Tool Founder" — "I was paying $250/mo for Churnkey when I had 40 subscribers. That was the whole problem. So I built the free version." Short, punchy, relatable — perfect for the Indie Bites format.
- **Warm-up:** Follow on Twitter, listen to recent episodes and mention a specific insight in your DM. Reference the episode title when you reach out.

### 3. Build Your SaaS (Jon Buda + Justin Jackson)
- **Audience size:** ~10,000 listeners/episode; bootstrapped SaaS community
- **Topic fit:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Talks extensively about pricing, growth, and the bootstrapper journey. Churn is a recurring topic.
- **Contact method:** Email jon@transistor.fm or reach Justin via Twitter (@mijustin). They have a feedback form on the show website.
- **Best angle:** "Bootstrapper's Take on Churn" — Position this as a research/data conversation. "I studied 20+ cancel flows from SaaS tools and found that most are either dark patterns or completely absent. Here's what I learned and what we built." Jon and Justin love the analytical, honest framing.
- **Warm-up:** Become a Transistor customer or mention using their product. Engage in their Discord community. Justin is active on Twitter — respond to his threads about pricing and revenue.

### 4. Starter Story (Pat Walls)
- **Audience size:** 45,000+ newsletter subscribers, 200k+ monthly website visitors
- **Topic fit:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Format is "how founders built their business." The ChurnRecovery origin story (couldn't afford Churnkey, built free alternative) is a classic Starter Story arc.
- **Contact method:** Submit via https://www.starterstory.com/share-your-story — it's a form-based submission. Also reachable on Twitter (@thepatwalls).
- **Best angle:** "Free Tool Founder" — The Starter Story audience loves bootstrapped origin stories. Frame it as: "From paying $250/mo for churn tooling to building a free version that now serves 500+ businesses on the waitlist."
- **Warm-up:** Submit the story form directly — Pat reads them. Simultaneously, engage on Twitter. Starter Story also runs "revenue milestones" posts — share yours when relevant.

## Pre-Pitch Checklist

### Episodes to Listen to First (2–3 per show)
Before pitching each show, listen to these specific types of episodes:
1. Their most recent "founder interview" episode
2. An episode specifically about monetization, pricing, or churn (search their archives)
3. An episode that went viral or has the most reviews — understand what their audience loves

For each episode you listen to, note:
- The format (interview length, question style)
- Specific jargon they use
- Topics they return to repeatedly
- Any gaps where ChurnRecovery would fit naturally

### Warm-Up Checklist (2 weeks before pitching)
- [ ] Follow the host on Twitter
- [ ] Subscribe to their newsletter (if they have one)
- [ ] Reply to 2–3 of their tweets with genuine, substantive comments
- [ ] Leave a review on Apple Podcasts or Spotify (mention a specific episode)
- [ ] Engage in any communities they run or participate in
- [ ] Reference their work in your own content (tweet, blog post, etc.)
- [ ] NOT: generic "love your show!" comments. Specific, valuable engagement only.

### Subject Line Formulas
- **Specific research hook:** "I analyzed [X] cancel flows — here's what I found"
- **Contrarian take:** "Why churn tools are priced wrong (and what I did about it)"
- **Gap identification:** "The $0 tool that solves a $250/mo problem for [audience]"
- **Origin story:** "I built [Tool] because [problem]. Would your audience care?"
- **Data-led:** "[Statistic] — would this make a useful episode?"
