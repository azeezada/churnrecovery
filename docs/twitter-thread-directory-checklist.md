## Tweet 2 (Why directories matter)

First: why bother with directories?

→ Free backlinks (DA 50–90)
→ Passive discovery traffic forever
→ Early social proof before you have users
→ Some platforms drive real trial signups

We've gotten 200+ waitlist visits from directories alone. Zero ad spend.

---

## Tweet 3 (AlternativeTo)

**AlternativeTo** — Start here.

Best for: Capturing people actively searching for alternatives to paid tools

What works: Add your product as an alternative to the expensive tool in your category. We listed ChurnRecovery on Churnkey's page.

Screenshots: 1280×800px. Show your dashboard, not a landing page.

Tip: Reply to reviews of competitor products. This shows up in search.

---

## Tweet 4 (G2)

**G2** — Slower ROI, but worth it.

Best for: B2B credibility. Showing up on "best [category] software" lists.

What works: Complete 100% of your profile before asking for reviews. Incomplete profiles don't rank.

Screenshots: 1600×900px minimum. Use real data or realistic mockups.

Tip: Even with zero reviews, a complete G2 profile builds trust when prospects Google you.

---

## Tweet 5 (Capterra)

**Capterra** — Similar to G2 but slightly easier to get listed.

Best for: SMB software buyers who search by category

What works: Nail the "About" section. First 2 sentences show in search results. Write for the searcher, not for yourself.

Screenshots: 1280×960px. Show the "before and after" if you can.

Tip: Capterra syndicates to Software Advice and GetApp. One submission = 3 listings.

---

## Tweet 6 (BetaList)

**BetaList** — Best for early traction + early adopters.

Best for: Pre-launch or just-launched products. Attracts people who love trying new tools.

What works: Clear one-liner ("free cancel flow for newsletters"). Don't oversell. BetaList audience is sophisticated.

Screenshots: 1200×800px. Show the product, not marketing copy.

Tip: Submit on Monday. Their newsletter goes out mid-week. Weekend submissions get buried.

---

## Tweet 7 (Indie Hackers)

**Indie Hackers** — Not a directory, but treat it like one.

Best for: Founders who are also your target users. Community-driven discovery.

What works: Write a real post about your journey. "Building a free Churnkey alternative" got us 300 profile views in 48 hours.

No screenshot spec — embed images directly in the post.

Tip: Reply to existing threads about churn/retention before posting. Be part of the community first.

---

## Tweet 8 (ProductHunt — timing)

**Product Hunt** — Save this for when you're ready.

Best for: One big launch moment. Drives a lot of traffic in 24 hours.

What works: Hunter who has followers matters. DM 3–5 hunters in your category a week before. Don't cold DM on launch day.

Screenshots: 1270×760px. You get up to 5. Use all 5.

Tip: Launch on Tuesday or Wednesday. Not Monday (too competitive). Not Friday (low traffic).

---

## Tweet 9 (Screenshot strategy)

Let's talk screenshots — because this is where most people underinvest.

Rules we learned:
→ One clear idea per screenshot
→ Use realistic data, not zeros
→ Dark gradient backgrounds > white
→ Export at 2x for retina
→ Show the outcome, not just the UI

Bad: "Here's our dashboard"
Good: "Here's $2,145 recovered this month"

---

## Tweet 10 (Copy that works)

Copy that converts on directory listings:

**One-liner formula:** [What you do] for [who] — [key differentiator]

Ours: "Free cancel flow tool for newsletter creators and subscription businesses — no monthly fee"

What to avoid:
❌ "Revolutionizing churn management"
❌ Buzzwords without specifics
❌ Burying the free/pricing angle

People scan. Lead with the thing that makes them click.

---

## Tweet 11 (Timing + sequencing)

Submission order matters:

**Week 1:** AlternativeTo + Capterra (quickest to get listed)
**Week 2:** G2 + BetaList
**Week 3:** IH post + soft launch
**Week 4+:** Product Hunt (after you have some social proof)

Don't do Product Hunt first. You want some reviews/upvotes somewhere before your big launch day.
