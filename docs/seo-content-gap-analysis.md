## Part 2: Competitor Content Gaps

### What Churnkey Has That We Don't

1. **Case studies with specific revenue numbers** — Churnkey publishes detailed case studies: "Company X recovered $47k in 90 days." We have no published case studies with real numbers yet. *Action: Prioritize getting 2–3 beta user case studies with permission to cite numbers.*

2. **"Cancel flow psychology" content** — Churnkey has blog posts on offer psychology (why discounts work, why pauses outperform discounts in SaaS). Our content is more tutorial-focused. *Action: Write "The Psychology Behind Cancel Flows That Actually Save Subscribers."*

3. **Monthly industry churn benchmarks** — Churnkey releases periodic "state of churn" data reports. These get links. We don't have anything similar yet. *Action: Create a quarterly churn benchmark post using aggregated industry data.*

4. **Podcast appearances and founder interviews** — Churnkey's founder has done podcast circuits. We have no press or podcast presence documented. *Action: Pitch 5 bootstrapper/SaaS podcasts using the press kit.*

5. **Integration-specific tutorials** — Churnkey has detailed "How to set up Churnkey with [Platform]" guides for each integration. We have landing pages but fewer step-by-step tutorials. *Action: Create tutorial posts for each major /for/ page.*

### What ProsperStack Publishes

1. **"Retention audit" content** — ProsperStack writes about auditing your churn funnel. We don't have this category yet. *Action: Create "Free Churn Recovery Audit Checklist" as a lead magnet + blog post.*

2. **SaaS-focused metrics content** — ProsperStack targets SaaS founders with content on LTV, CAC, and churn interplay. *Action: This is a content gap — we target non-technical audiences but can add depth.*

3. **Customer success resources** — ProsperStack has guides on customer success as a churn prevention strategy. *Action: Write "Customer Success Playbook for Small Subscription Businesses."*

### What Neither Covers That We Should Own

1. **Creator economy churn content** — Neither Churnkey nor ProsperStack meaningfully covers newsletter creators, Substack, Ghost, Kajabi, or Patreon churn. This is our blue ocean. *High priority.*

2. **"Free alternative" comparison content** — No competitor writes "free alternative to [tool]" because they are the paid tool. We are uniquely positioned to own this category.

3. **Non-technical founder audience** — Both Churnkey and ProsperStack write for technical users. We can own the "non-technical business owner" voice entirely.

4. **Platform-migration churn guides** — "What happens to your subscribers when you move from Paddle to Stripe?" Nobody covers this. We should.

5. **Bootstrapped founder SaaS economics** — Content that explains churn economics specifically for solo founders and micro-SaaS operators. Underserved.
