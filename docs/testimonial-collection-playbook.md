# Testimonial Collection Playbook — ChurnRecovery
*Created: March 2026*

> **Goal:** Get 5–10 real, specific, outcome-based testimonials from early users within the first 60 days. Display them everywhere a prospect might be on the fence.

---

## 1. When to Ask

**Timing is everything.** Ask too early and they haven't seen results. Ask too late and the moment has passed. Hit these three windows:

### Window 1 — After First Successful Churn Recovery Event ⭐ Best timing
- **Trigger:** A subscriber was about to cancel and the cancel flow saved them — OR — a failed payment was recovered via dunning
- **How to know:** Dashboard shows a "recovery" event for that user
- **Why it works:** This is their "holy sh*t it actually works" moment. Emotions are high, results are fresh, they want to share.

### Window 2 — After 2 Weeks of Active Use
- **Trigger:** User has had ChurnRecovery installed and active for 14 days
- **How to know:** Check onboarding date in your user database
- **Why it works:** Enough time to see real data, not enough time to forget the pain before ChurnRecovery

### Window 3 — After a Positive Mention in Public
- **Trigger:** Someone mentions ChurnRecovery positively in a community (IH, Discord, Reddit, Twitter/X)
- **How to know:** Set up Google Alerts for "ChurnRecovery" and monitor communities you're active in
- **Why it works:** They already said it publicly — you're just asking them to say it more formally

### ❌ Don't Ask:
- During onboarding (too early — no results yet)
- After a support ticket (they're frustrated)
- More than once without a result change (annoying)
- In a mass email blast to your whole list (feels impersonal)

## 2. How to Ask

Keep asks short, specific, and personal. One paragraph max. Link directly to a Google Form or ask for a reply.

---

### Template A — Email Ask

**Subject:** Quick question about ChurnRecovery

```
Hey [First Name],

Noticed [you just recovered your first subscriber / you've been using ChurnRecovery for 2 weeks] — hope it's been working well.

If ChurnRecovery has helped you retain subscribers or recover payments, would you be willing to share a 2–3 sentence quote about what you've seen? I'd love to display it on the site to help other [newsletter creators / coaches / SaaS founders] who are on the fence.

Takes 3 minutes: [Google Form link] or just reply to this email.

No worries if not — appreciate you either way.

— Dawood
```

> **Personalization:** Replace bracketed parts. Mention the specific recovery event if you have the data (e.g., "you recovered 3 subscribers last week").

---

```
Hey [Name]! Glad ChurnRecovery is working for you 🙌

Or just reply here — I'll turn it into a quote and send it back for approval before posting anything.
```

## 3. What to Ask For

Don't ask open-ended "can you say something nice?" questions. Give them specific prompts that produce **outcome-based** quotes — the kind that actually move a skeptical reader.

### Prompts That Get Usable Testimonials

Use 1–2 of these. More than 2 is overwhelming.

**Outcome prompts (best for homepage display):**
- *"What specific result have you seen since installing ChurnRecovery?"*
- *"How many subscribers or payments have you recovered in the last [X weeks]?"*
- *"Before ChurnRecovery, what was your monthly churn rate? Has it changed?"*

**Comparison prompts (best for comparison pages):**
- *"What were you using before ChurnRecovery — or what were you considering? Why did you choose this instead?"*
- *"Were you skeptical about trying a free tool? What changed your mind?"*

**Recommendation prompts (best for trust/social proof):**
- *"What would you tell another [newsletter creator / online coach / SaaS founder] who's losing subscribers but hasn't tried ChurnRecovery yet?"*
- *"If a friend asked you 'should I bother setting this up?', what would you say?"*

### What "Usable" Means

| ❌ Not usable | ✅ Usable |
|---------------|-----------|
| "Great tool! Love it." | "Recovered 3 subscribers in the first week — made back $180 I would have lost." |
| "Really helpful, easy to use." | "Setup took 10 minutes. I was skeptical it would actually work, but it caught my first cancellation attempt the next day." |
| "Highly recommend!" | "I was paying $250/month for Churnkey for the same thing. This is exactly what a bootstrapped creator needs." |

The difference: **specifics beat superlatives**. Numbers, timeframes, comparisons.

## 4. Testimonial Formats

Collect testimonials in multiple formats. Different formats work better in different placements.

### Format 1 — Text Quote (most versatile)
- 2–5 sentences, first person, outcome-focused
- Works everywhere: homepage, landing pages, comparison pages, emails
- What to collect: reply email text or Google Form response
- How to display: blockquote with name, company type, headshot (optional)

### Format 2 — Tweet Screenshot
- Screenshot of a real tweet or reply mentioning ChurnRecovery
- Works well: homepage, Product Hunt gallery, Twitter ads
- How to collect: screenshot a tweet + save the URL
- **Only use public tweets.** Always DM to confirm they're okay with it being used in marketing.

### Format 3 — Loom Video (30 seconds)
- Short screen recording or face-to-camera
- Highest trust signal. Most powerful for homepage hero section.
- Ask: *"Would you be willing to record a 30-second Loom about your experience? Just casual — no script needed."*
- Works best for case studies, homepage featured testimonial, LinkedIn

### Format 4 — Case Study (long form)
- 300–500 words: problem, solution, specific results
- Best for: dedicated case study page, blog post, guest content
- Template available at `docs/case-study-template.md`
- Ask users who have clear before/after metrics (subscriber count, churn rate, monthly revenue saved)

## 5. Where to Display

### Homepage Trust Section
- 3–6 short text quotes, displayed under the hero or above the CTA
- Mix of user types (newsletter creator, SaaS founder, coach)
- Prioritize quotes with numbers and comparisons

### Comparison Pages (`/compare/churnkey`, etc.)
- Testimonials from users who explicitly switched from or considered the competitor
- Use the "comparison prompt" testimonials here

### Landing Pages (`/for/substack`, `/for/coaches`, etc.)
- Use testimonials from that exact audience segment
- A Substack creator testimonial on the Substack landing page = much higher relevance

### Product Hunt Gallery
- Screenshot of tweet testimonials
- Loom video if you have one
- Add 2–3 strong text quotes in the PH description

### Email Drip Sequence
- Drop 1–2 testimonials in the welcome email series
- "Here's what other newsletter creators are saying after week one..."

## 6. Incentive Strategy

Most early users will give you a testimonial if you just ask — especially right after a positive experience. But having something to offer helps.

### What to Offer (in order of preference)

| Incentive | When to Offer | What It Costs You |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Extended free tier** | Anyone in early access | Nothing upfront; goodwill |
| **Public thanks** | Default for everyone | 0 — tweet their name, link their newsletter |
| **Feature credit** | Power users, early adopters | Prioritize their feature request |
| **Co-marketing** | Users with audiences (newsletter, podcast, community) | Your time for a collaboration |
| **Case study feature** | Users with strong metrics | Helps them too — "as featured on ChurnRecovery" |

### What NOT to Do
- Don't pay for reviews on G2/Capterra — violates ToS and they'll remove them
- Don't offer cash for testimonials — creates legal issues and the testimonial reads fake
- Don't promise features you haven't built yet

### Default Offer (include in your ask):
*"As a thank-you, I'll add you to our early-access extended free tier, plus I'll shout out [their newsletter/product] when I share the testimonial publicly."*

## 7. Legal Note

⚠️ **Always get written permission before displaying a testimonial with someone's name, photo, or company.**

### Minimum Requirements
Before publishing any testimonial publicly (website, social, ads):

1. **Written confirmation** — An email reply ("Yes, you can use this") counts. Keep it.
2. **Name and attribution** — Confirm exactly how they want to be credited (full name, first name only, company name)
3. **Approval of final wording** — If you edited their quote for length, send them the final version to approve before posting
4. **Twitter/public posts** — Confirm they're okay with screenshots being used in marketing, even if the tweet is public

### Simple Permission Ask
When submitting the form or replying:
> *"By submitting this, you're giving ChurnRecovery permission to display your quote on our website and marketing materials, credited as [Name, Company]. Reply to this email to confirm — and let me know if you'd prefer to be listed differently (first name only, anonymous, etc.)."*

### No Need for a Lawyer (at this stage)
For a pre-revenue early-stage product, a clear email confirmation is sufficient. If you raise money or run paid ads using testimonials, consult a lawyer to formalize the process.
